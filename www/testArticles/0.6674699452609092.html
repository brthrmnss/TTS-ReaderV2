Grenades.

This scene might look familiar to most of you.

Our beloved action hero/ villain grabs a hand grenade, pulls the pin, and throws it.

And a giant fire ball follows as a result of the explosion.

Yea... this never happens.

A frag grenade does not explode into a massive fire ball, hence the name fragmentation grenade and not “super fireball explosion grenade III”.

Upon detonation, the frag grenade unleashes approximately 1,024 (depends on the manufacturer) of lethal fragments, propelling them in every possible direction.

The grenades lethality is the thousands of flying steel pellets, not the actual explosion.

Unless you are throwing a frag grenade into a car’s gas tank, underneath a car, or into a house with a massive gas leak, I would expect the explosion to look more like this:

Tommy Holland, studied at University of Memphis
Answered Jan 23

The ways people get killed. People die off way too easily and way too quickly in movies. And if they are an essential character, they die too slowly. I'll go into detail…

The neck snap is probably the worst of all. It just isn't even remotely realistic. It's usually in action films and used frequently. The good guy sneaks up on some minor bad character. To silence this character and remove him, he snaps the character's neck. CRACK! One sharp move and the guy is a limp dead body. This would NEVER occur in reality. It is almost impossible to snap another person's neck like that- people bend and move easily with their neck; their spine would twist with them and their neck would be harmed, but not broken. To effectively snap a man's neck you would need at least two people and they would have to be INCREDIBLY strong. You'd have to hold his back completely still and then have the power to violently twist his head until his cervical vertebra snap… yeah, not happening. I would love to see a realistic reaction to an attempted neck snapping, in which the intended victim is like "aww, that was very uncomfortable!".

The slit throat is another improbable death. In order for it to be effective, you have to basically half-sever someone's head. A simple slice across the neck will need stitches, but won't kill someone. To kill someone via slit throat, you gotta dig in deep- it takes longer than a fast slash- you've really gotta want it! And even then, death is not instantaneous- there will be a lot of convulsing and gurgling. To do it fully, you will more than likely also cut into the victim's trachea, resulting in a terrible wheezing sound as the poor fucker struggles to breathe through a hole in his windpipe. So again, it's not fast and it's NOT quiet. So dispatching a person via cut throat is not the simple process seen in movies.

Gunshots are often lethal in movies whereas in reality, most people survive gun shots. And shots to the head, while effective, require very good aim- aim that most people in a gun fight are going to have trouble with.

Stabbing is similar to gun shots and people are even more likely to survive them.

Also, things being blown up- most things don't explode like they do in movies, so I also snicker when a person crashes a car, and it explodes. Get real.

Choking someone is another tough one to swallow (pardon my pun!)- it's incredibly hard to strangle someone- you would need to be incredibly strong and the victim incapacitated. A grown man strangling another unrestrained and fully conscious grown man is probably not going to successfully kill him. People can violently swing away- so strangling is very difficult, even with a garrote.

Im not saying it isn't possible to die these ways- it's just not as easy as the movies make it appear.

Answered Jan 24

Every cheesy romantic movie scene is basically non-existent in real life. They just exist to stimulate your hopes on the power of love!

You can have a romantic moment with your significant other, which requires a little preparation and often, these things work out ok, but sometimes they don’t go exactly as planned because real people and the things they do are imperfect…

…but in rom-coms, people do extremely weird things, that always end up perfectly, and apparently everyone the in-movie world sees them as normal and even encourage them, like:

  • Running through an airport to reach your loved one before they board the plane, so you can intensely confess your love and beg them to stay (with everyone on the plane -including flight attendants- clapping and celebrating your bravery and love); sometimes even breaching security and ilegally boarding the plane. In real life, your loved one will think you’re a weirdo, people on the plane will film you and upload the video with the caption “OMG crazy guy illegally boarded the plane and started screaming to a girl, probably a terrorist!” you’ll be arrested and your name will possibly be written on the no-fly list.
  • Interrupting a wedding, to reveal some ground breaking truth and prevent the bride from marrying; instead making her kiss you in front of everybody; sometimes with the bride or someone else punching the groom… In real life… well, try that and tell me how it goes LOL. Also, gossip and mobile technology can make people communicate important stuff almost instantly without you having to race to a church and ruin people’s day.
  • Magically converting a space (room, restaurant) in a romantic spot using like 1000 big candles. Do you know how unsafe that is? Do you know how expensive candles are? There are some hotels/restaurants that can provide that service to you, though, but you have to pay and it won’t be prepared in just minutes (I used said service when I proposed to my then girlfriend/now wife, but still, it needed some planning and coordination between me and hotel staff for days, and while there were candles, I don’t think they were more than 30 :P).
  • Giving long, meaningful, romantic speeches in a totally spontaneous way. When I proposed, I had a bunch on romantic things in my head to say when kneeling, but when I saw my girlfriend overwhelmed in tears of joy watching the ring, I crumbled and couldn’t remember a single thing! I only managed to pop the question with a tearful, broken voice and that was it (not that it made the moment any less special). And my wedding vows were carefully redacted, revised, redacted again, and again until I came with a final version. No way I could have made them up in the moment.
  • Being enemies with someone and not long after that, in the heat of an argument, kiss/have sex passionately. Sorry, if someone hates you, don’t count of them kissing or lusting after you anytime soon… with some time and work from both sides, you can become cordial acquaintances, even friends, but “haters to lovers” is basically impossible.
